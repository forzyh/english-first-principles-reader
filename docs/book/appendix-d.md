# 附录D　参考答案与解析

开放题提供一种可行答案。只要结构关系、参照点和信息功能一致，其他自然表达也可成立。

## D.1 C.1—C.2 参考答案

**1.** 简化树：

```text
Sentence
├── Subject NP
│   ├── Det: The
│   ├── Modifier: careful
│   └── Head N: technician
└── Predicate VP
    ├── Modifier: quickly
    ├── Head V: restarted
    └── Object NP
        ├── Det: the
        ├── Modifier: damaged
        └── Head N: server
```

careful 与 damaged 分别挂在两个名词节点，quickly 挂在重启事件上。

**2.**

```text
[Several [highly experienced] engineers [from our Singapore office]]
[have joined [the project]].
```

主语名词短语中心为 engineers；形容词短语中心为 experienced，highly 修饰它；介词短语 from... 后置修饰 engineers。谓语中心词是 joined，have 提供现在完成层。

**3.** 两种挂接：

- 鸟在阳台上：She photographed the bird that was on the balcony.
- 她在阳台上拍照：While she was on the balcony, she photographed the bird.

原句的 on the balcony 可挂在 bird 或 photographed 上。

**4.**

- The storm 是句法主语，也是原因/施事样角色；
- The bridge 是句法主语、语义 patient；the storm 在 by 短语中恢复原因角色；
- there 是存在结构的占位主语，several cracks 是被引入的存在实体；
- it 是天气结构的占位主语，没有具体指称。

**5.**

- in the drawer 是 put 的位置补语；put 通常需要目标位置。
- 对 find 而言该地点不是必要槽位，因此通常是修饰语；它可修饰发现事件，也可在某些语境中限定 keys。
- angry 是主语补语，完成 became 的结果状态框架。
- angrily 是方式修饰语，删除后 He spoke 仍形成完整事件。

**6.** 一种生长过程：

```text
A researcher found an error.
A young researcher found an error last night.
A young researcher from the data quality team found an error in the training data last night.
A young researcher from the data quality team found an error in the training data last night that had caused the model to produce biased results.
```

最后一句的关系从句容易被误挂到 last night，更清楚的版本是：

> Last night, a young researcher from the data quality team found an error in the training data. The error had caused the model to produce biased results.

或：

> A young researcher from the data quality team discovered last night that an error in the training data had caused the model to produce biased results.

这里展示了一个重要原则：树能继续生长，不代表必须永远保持单句。

**7.** 两句都合语法。第一句用占位 it 先满足短主语接口，把较重的 that 从句放到末端，更符合 end weight 和 end focus；第二句把内容从句主题化，适合该命题已经是当前讨论对象的语境。

**8.** 可能语境与完整版本：

- 看完方案后评价：The proposal looks promising.
- 回答“要不要等”：There is no need to wait. / We do not need to wait.
- 邀请同行：Are you coming with us?
- 回答进度询问：I have already finished. / It is already finished.

片段自然，是因为人物、对象或时态可由对话恢复。

**9.**

- S+V；
- S+V+C；
- S+V+O+O；
- S+V+O+C；
- S+V+O。

**10.**

- She explained the process to me.
- We discussed the delay.
- He suggested that we leave early. 或 He suggested leaving early.
- They prevented the visitors from entering.
- I entered the building.

这些修复都来自谓语接口，而非随机增删介词。

**11.**

- 不及物变化：The door opened slowly.
- 及物使动：Maya opened the door.
- 状态：The door is open.

三者分别构形为变化事件、使动事件和结果/属性状态。

**12.**

> I saw **an** unusual device on the desk. **The** device had no visible buttons, but **the** screen on its side was glowing. I asked **an** engineer nearby what it was. She said it was used to measure **Ø** air quality.

an device 首次引入；the device 恢复同一对象；the screen 由设备部件框架与后置描述唯一定位；an engineer 引入任一附近工程师；air quality 是不可数抽象概念的普通用法。

**13.**

- Children 泛指儿童类别；the children 指语境中可识别的一组儿童。
- go to hospital 在英式英语中可表示以病人身份参与医院制度；go to the hospital 指前往可识别医院。在美式英语中制度性用法也常带 the。
- became president 把 president 当作职位补语；the president 指可识别的任职者。
- Coffee 泛指材料/商品类别；the coffee I bought 由关系从句限定为特定咖啡。

**14.** 原句中两个 she 都可能指 Maya 或 Lena。清晰版本取决于意图：

> After Lena received the results, Maya called her. Maya sounded relieved.

或：

> After Lena received the results, Maya called her, and Lena sounded relieved.

重复名字比模糊代词更有效。

**15.**

> A customer entered the shop. **The customer** asked for a refund. The assistant checked **the receipt**, but **it** was missing a date. Then **the customer** called the manager.

第一句创建 customer，后文恢复；receipt 是该退款事件中被递交的特定收据。

**16.**

- I bought a bicycle yesterday.
- The bicycle is surprisingly light. / It is surprisingly light.
- Could you close the door?
- Bicycles are an efficient form of transport.

## D.2 C.3 参考答案

**17.**

- at the station：车站作为坐标；
- in the station：车站作为内部空间；
- on Monday morning：日期/日程面；
- at 8:30：时间点；
- at risk：处于风险坐标；
- in trouble：处于问题状态范围。

**18.**

- through the gate；
- into the room；
- through the tunnel；
- across the river；
- toward the teacher；
- out of the window。

**19.**

- on the desk 挂在名词 report 上；
- on the train 挂在事件 read 上；
- In my view 挂在整个命题上，标记立场；
- to delay the launch 修饰/补足名词 decision，on Friday 挂在 was announced 事件上。

**20.**

- 只有经理批准，排除其他人；
- 经理只做了“批准”这一行为，可能未修改或推动；
- 经理只批准计划，未批准其他对象；
- 直到昨天才批准，焦点在时间。口语重音会进一步确定解释。

**21.**

- After reviewing the data, the analysts found several inconsistencies.
- As I walked through the lab, the alarm suddenly sounded.
- To reduce costs, the company replaced the office lights.

修复要为前置非谓语或目的结构提供合适的控制主语。

**22.**

- 名词性从句，占主句主语槽位；what 在内部占 discovered 的宾语角色。
- 定语从句，挂在 error 上；that 与 discovered 后的宾语空位绑定。
- 状语从句，挂在 changed 事件上，提供原因。
- 名词性从句，填 wonder 的内容槽位；whether 建立是非变量。

**23.**

- The engineers who work in our Berlin office completed the audit. 限定性，不加逗号。
- Dr. Chen, who leads our Berlin office, completed the audit. 补充性，加逗号。
- I reviewed the report that you sent yesterday. 宾语关系词也可省略：the report you sent yesterday。

**24.**

- Do you know where the file is?
- I wonder why the system failed.
- Could you tell me when the meeting will start?

主句承担疑问操作，嵌入从句使用陈述语序。

## D.3 C.4 参考答案

**25.**

- finished：yesterday 建立封闭过去参照框架；
- have finished：从现在回看，结果支持“现在可以发送”；
- have received：this week 尚开放，当前时点是参照中心；
- received：last week 已封闭；
- has lived：状态从 2020 延续到现在。

**26.**

- I lived... 把五年居住期封闭在过去，通常暗示现在不住在马德里；
- I have lived... 从现在回看，通常表示仍住在那里或该经历仍属于当前时间框架；
- I had lived... 从“搬到里斯本”这一过去参照点回看更早的五年居住期。

**27.**

- boils：普遍规律；
- is boiling：当前过程；
- am reading：本周正在展开的临时项目；
- read：习惯；
- are always interrupting：进行体加 always 表示带态度的反复行为。

**28.**

- has written：突出已完成产出“三章”；
- has been writing：突出持续过程和时长；
- has been raining：突出刚刚持续的过程及当前痕迹；
- have solved：突出当前可用的结果。若强调长期努力，也可说 have been working on the problem，但不能与 solve 机械替换。

**29.**

列车先离开：

> When we arrived at the station, the train **had already left**, so we called a taxi.

列车后离开：

> We arrived at the station, but the train left before we could board, so we called a taxi.

或明确先到：

> After we **had arrived** at the station, the train left unexpectedly, so we called a taxi.

第二种语境中时间顺序可由 after 和叙事顺序表达，过去完成并非强制。

**30.**

- I’ll get it.：即时决定；
- I’m meeting the client on Thursday.：已安排会面；
- It’s going to rain.：基于当前迹象；
- The train leaves at six tomorrow morning.：固定时刻表。

**31.**

- think 表示持有观点，是状态；
- am thinking 表示当前进行的思考过程；
- is kind 描述较稳定特征；
- is being kind 把“友善”重新构形为当前临时行为。

**32.**

> The rain **was falling**, and people **were hurrying** home when a car suddenly **stopped** beside me.

两个过去进行体建立背景，简单过去把突然停车作为前景事件推进时间轴。

## D.4 C.5 参考答案

**33.**

- 认识情态：根据灯亮推断；
- 义务情态：规则要求；
- 动力/能力情态；
- 认识情态：低承诺预测；
- 动力/倾向意义：门在冬天有时会卡住。

**34.**

- must not leave：必要性作用于否定命题，表示禁止离开；
- do not have to leave：否定必要性，表示可以走也可以不走；
- need not have left：实际上离开了，但事后看没有必要；
- should not have left：实际上离开了，说话者认为这是错误或不合适的行为。

**35.**

- 低把握可能：She might have forgotten the appointment.
- 强推断：She must have forgotten the appointment.
- 过去批评：She should not have forgotten the appointment.
- 过去不必要但已发生：形式上可说 She need not have forgotten the appointment.，但语用上不自然，因为“忘记”通常不是有意执行、也很少被评价为“没有必要”。这道题说明语法形式可组合，不代表命题在现实中自然。更典型的例子是 She need not have attended the meeting.

**36.**

- If it rains tomorrow, we will stay home.
- If I knew the answer, I would tell you.
- If they had tested the backup, they would have discovered the fault.
- If I had taken that job, I would be living abroad now.

**37.** 一条可能的距离梯度：

> Please send me the revised file today.

> Could you send me the revised file today?

> Would it be possible for you to send me the revised file today?

具体场景中并非越间接越好；紧急或职责明确时，第一版可能最清楚。

**38.**

- The team did not complete the test.
- Did the team complete the test?
- Who completed the test?
- What did the team complete?
- It was the test that the team completed. 或口语重音：The team completed the TEST.

**39.**

- Does she like tea?
- Where did she go?
- He cannot swim. / He can’t swim.
- Did they complete the test?
- I don’t know where he is.

第1、4句的问题是时态/一致在助动词与词汇动词上重复；第3句已有情态助动词，不再使用 do；第5句是嵌入疑问，不倒装。

**40.**

- The element was discovered in 1898. 如施事重要可保留 by scientists。
- My laptop was stolen last night.
- The application will be reviewed next week.
- The machine was repaired with a new component.

被动让前文对象继续占据主语位置，并可省略未知或不重要的施事。

## D.5 C.6 参考答案

**41.** 合理顺序是 B—D—E—A—C：

> The team installed a new monitoring system. **The system** began collecting detailed error logs. **The logs** showed a pattern of repeated failures. **These failures** revealed a memory leak. **The leak** occurred when the service processed unusually large files.

每句把前句的新对象提升为下句的已知起点。

**42.**

- The storm damaged the roof. 焦点是施事 the storm。
- The storm damaged the roof. 在口语中重读 roof；也可答 It damaged the roof.
- No. It was the roof that the storm damaged. 显式纠正对比。

同一语序在不同问答中可通过重音调整，但被动或分裂句可在书面语中更清楚地编码焦点。

**43.**

- 因果/推论：Because the sample was small, the result should be interpreted cautiously.
- 让步：Although the sample was small, the effect was statistically significant.
- 推理证据：The lights are on, so Maya must be home. 或 Maya must be home because the lights are on.
- 开放条件：If you submit the form today, we can process it this week.

**44.** 主句：

> The model **was deployed** after the final audit…

定语从句：

> that the team had trained __ on the revised dataset

它修饰 model，关系词对应 trained 的宾语空位。had trained 是过去完成，表示训练先于部署。was deployed 是被动。原因从句是：

> because its error rate had been reduced to an acceptable level

其中 had been reduced 同时是过去完成与被动，表示错误率降低先于部署，并构成部署原因。

**45.** 两种作用域：

- 没有批准，原因是昂贵：Because the plan was expensive, the manager did not approve it.
- 批准了，但不是因为昂贵：The manager approved the plan, but not because it was expensive.

原句中的否定可覆盖 approve，也可只否定原因关系。

**46.**

- 主干/存在结构错误：There are three options.
- 槽位错误：She suggested that I wait. / She suggested waiting.
- 挂接/控制错误：Having finished the analysis, we sent the report to the client.
- 事件元数据冲突：I met her last year.
- 引用歧义：按意图改为 Maya told Lena, “I need more time.”，或 Maya told Lena that Lena needed more time.
- 篇章逻辑错误。若意图是降价带来需求上升：The price decreased; therefore, demand rose because customers found the product cheaper. 若成本上升，通常需要另建因果链，例如：Production costs increased, so the company raised the price, and demand fell.

**47.** 一种答案：

> An engineer installed **a new sensor** yesterday. **The sensor** detects temperature changes with high accuracy.

第一句用 a 创建对象，第二句用 the 恢复，并把新属性放在谓语末端。

**48.** 示例段落：

> The present perfect cannot be reduced to the Chinese word “已经,” because its central job is to connect an earlier situation to a current reference point. Consider the contrast between *I finished the report yesterday* and *I have finished the report*. The first sentence places the event inside a closed past period, whereas the second presents the completed report as relevant to the situation now. The same reference-point logic also explains *She has lived here for ten years*, where the state began in the past and continues into the present. However, not every past event with a present consequence requires the present perfect; speakers can choose the simple past when they frame the event as part of a finished narrative. A closed expression such as *yesterday* normally reinforces that past frame. The useful question, therefore, is not whether the Chinese translation contains “已经,” but where the speaker places the reference point.

该段落以 the present perfect 为中心对象，随后用 the first sentence / the second 建立对比，再把 the same reference-point logic 作为已知起点引出持续用法，最后加入限制并回扣判断问题。
